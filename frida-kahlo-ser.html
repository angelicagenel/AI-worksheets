Cultural Information

Frida Kahlo created approximately 200 paintings during her artistic career, with about 55 being self-portraits. Her most famous works include "The Two Fridas" (1939), "Self-Portrait with Thorn Necklace and Hummingbird" (1940), and "What the Water Gave Me" (1938). Each painting tells a story about her life, emotions, and Mexican identity. Frida used vibrant colors typical of Mexican folk art and incorporated indigenous symbols from pre-Columbian cultures. Her paintings are displayed in museums worldwide, with the Frida Kahlo Museum in Mexico City (Casa Azul) being the most important collection of her work.
