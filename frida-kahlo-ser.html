Cultural Information

Frida Kahlo created approximately 200 paintings during her artistic career, with about 55 being self-portraits. Her most famous works include "The Two Fridas" (1939), "Self-Portrait with Thorn Necklace and Hummingbird" (1940), and "What the Water Gave Me" (1938). Each painting tells a story about her life, emotions, and Mexican identity. Frida used vibrant colors typical of Mexican folk art and incorporated indigenous symbols from pre-Columbian cultures. Her paintings are displayed in museums worldwide, with the Frida Kahlo Museum in Mexico City (Casa Azul) being the most important collection of her work.

Answer Key

1. "Las Dos Fridas" es su obra más famosa.
Answer: es
SER for identity/classification - we use SER to say what something IS.
2. El cuadro "Autorretrato con Collar de Espinas" es muy simbólico.
Answer: es
SER for characteristics - we use SER to describe what something IS like (permanent qualities).
3. Los colores en sus pinturas son muy vibrantes y tradicionales.
Answer: son
SER for characteristics - we use SER to describe inherent qualities and characteristics.
4. Muchas de sus obras son óleos sobre lienzo.
Answer: son
SER for material/composition - we use SER to say what something is made of.
5. Yo soy estudiante de arte y admiro mucho a Frida.
Answer: soy
SER for profession/identity - we use SER to say what someone's profession or identity is.
6. Nosotros somos admiradores de la cultura mexicana.
Answer: somos
SER for identity/characteristics - we use SER to say what we ARE (our identity or role).
7. Tú eres muy creativo, como Frida Kahlo.
Answer: eres
SER for characteristics - we use SER to describe permanent personality traits and qualities.
8. Las pinturas de Frida están en museos importantes del mundo.
Answer: están
ESTAR for location - we use ESTAR to say WHERE something is located or positioned.
